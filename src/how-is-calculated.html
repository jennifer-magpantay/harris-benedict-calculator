How calories are calculated?

The estimate total calories a person needs, or as said before the Total Energy Expenditure (TEE), takes in consideration two parameters: the Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and Physical Activities Level (PAL) of an individual.

How BMR is Calculated Using the Harris-Benedict Equation?

According to Harris-Benedict equation, the estimates BMR rates values are based on height, weight, age, and gender of an individual. The resulting number represents about 60% to 75% of the daily kilocalorie intake to maintain current body weight, because it does not include the calories expended in activities and exercise.

Harris-Benedict Equation

The original Harris–Benedict equations was published in 1919. In 1984, a revision made by by Roza and Shizgal improved its accuracy.

Harris-Benedict Equation revised by Roza and Shizgal
Gender Weight Height Age
Male 88.362 + (13.397 × weight in kg) + (4.799 × height in cm) - (5.677 × age in years)
Female - (5.677 × age in years) + (3.098 × height in cm) + (3.098 × height in cm)

The equation adjusts the BMR to reflect activity levels by multiplying the values by one of a set of activity factors multipliers. The factors reflect the levels of activity throughout a one-week period as showed below:

Activity Factors Multipliers
Level of Activity Example Activity Factor
Sedentary Little to no exercise, such as a desk job with no additional physical activity 1.2
Lightly active Light exercise 1-2 days/week 1.375
Moderatelly active Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week 1.55
Very Active Hard exercise 6-7 days/week 1.725
Extremely Active Hard daily exercise and physical job or two times a day training 1.9
